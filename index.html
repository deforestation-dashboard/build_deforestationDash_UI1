What Is Deforestation?

Explaination

Forests are important, they help keep our climate stable by absorbing things like carbon dioxide, while releasing back oxygen. Trees also help regulate the water table and provide food an nutrition for many animals and people around the world. Many animal use them for shelter and protection against predators and extreme weather. There is a rich variety of life that is dependent on forest to keep them safe, shetered and fed.

Concern

Over 1 billion people live near and rely directly on forest and timber for fuel, food, medicines, and building materials. And globaly the demand for timber products is projected to more than tripple over the next three decades. This problem that has gotten out of hand; so much so that a football field worth of forest is lost every second (in 2017). This problem is an urgent concern for not only the animals that live in and around these Woodedlands but also for humans both the live near or far from forest. We have a responsiblity to mantain and uphold this great earth and be good stewards of it.

Why Are We Destroying So Many Trees?

Factors

There are many causes to deforestation, but the main problem is lack of forest management by local authorities and citizens. Forest are a very important part of many eco system, but many times these forests are converted to support agricultural demand for pulp, palm and soy plantations. Without proper planning and regulation this can spiral into a large scale problem quickly. Another large factor to deforestation is logging. Though responsible logging takes time and effort to properly assess each site before removing trees, then replacing the trees with new trees, which will benefit the forest. Irresponsible logging takes trees without regard for environmental effects and doesnt worry about how this will effect for forest in the future. By miss managing forest we have let this problem get out of hand.

If you lead the charge to help drive change others will follow you and want to make a difference which will better every as a whole. As a steward of global change to better the earth you must stay well informed on the issues, how it is changing and evolving, and what we are doing to help change the world for the better (or worst).
